BRIDGEGOOD: Portfolio Feature Redesign

The Problem

BRIDGEGOOD offers a platform for creatives to display their work. An issue we noticed among our user’s was the lack of activity on a user portfolio page after they've created an account and uploaded one project. We began to make hypothesis, concluding that one of the reasons for this could be the overall interaction with the portfolio feature not sufficing the needs of our users.

Our Users

Creatives on BRIDGEGOOD, localized communities and businesses, other designers who visit the BRIDGEGOOD platform.

The Solution

Our solutions made the portfolio experience a lot more intuitive for our users. We minimize the amount of branding on the project display page (that follows the rebranding of BRIDGEGOOD) and displayed the projects in a manner that excites users to upload their work. Creatives want to showcase their work and display it in a way that makes them proud of their creations. This solution enabled our users to exercise that feeling.

The Research

User Interviews

My team and I conducted user interviews of the BRIDGEGOOD creatives who use our platform. The most pivotal insights received from our users were:

01.

Lack of a Personal Connection

Users felt that the BRIDGEGOOD branding overpowered the work displayed on the their portfolio page. This made them feel that their work wasn't owned by them.

02.

Monotonus Display

Users were dissatisfied with how the display of their work was being handled. Small square thumbnails were very underwhelming to our users. In addition, a common theme we saw in our research from user interviews was that users wanted a way to highlight their best project piece. BRIDGEGOOD did have such feature implimented.

03.

Lack of Opportunity to Tell Their Story

Researching these companies has enabled our team to understand functioning trends. Looking into Dribble and Behance, the portfolio website products, some of the key functions that we saw work best were the variations in sizes in regards to the display of their user's projects. Larger displays seemed to be most popular. As for Squarespace and Adobe Portfolio, the website builder products, we were able to identify common preferences in regards to style and location of their user's content and images.

Next Steps

Now that we have decent feedback on our redesign, we plan to continue to gather data of the activity/retention rate on the BRIDGEGOOD platform. If we see negative trends or behaviors, steps of improvement sha'll follow shortly after.

Conclusion

Something that I really took from this was that user needs play a impactful role on a portfolio display. Performing research and gaining user insights shows that creatives enjoy having displayable canvases that encompass their personalities, which is a key concept to relaying their style and passion towards a viewing audience. Here, on the BRIDGEGOOD platform, we ensure that concept by creating a better experience for our users.
