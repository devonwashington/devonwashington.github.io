Here's a little bit about me

I am currently a student at San Jose State University going on my final year as a BA - Graphic Design student. I was previously a UX Design Apprenctice at BRIDGEGOOD, where I learned various fundemental skills within UX that I utilize everyday. Now, I am a UX/UI Intern at Restvo where I am doing the things that I love; creating better experiences for users.

My path of design started at a very young age. From the age of four, I would've always been creating some type of contraption. These contraptions ranged from drawings, lego buildings, and sometimes crayon doodles on the walls at home! This passion for creating carried on throughout grade school, in which I took many different art related classes. It wasn't until college I truly began understanding the importance of design. From this point onward, my interest in human-center design sparked.

Design is my life, but it isn't my only outlet for creative expression. I have been breakdancing at a regional-level for almost a decade, and I have learning the Korean language for over two years now. I utilize these two passions, especially dance, as a technique to connect myself with the physical world through a creative lense. This allows me to reach beyond systems of traditional design to think in different ways to solve problems within user experience and vice versa.
